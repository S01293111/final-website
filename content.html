What is Climate Change?

Climate change is the phrase used to describe the way Earth is negatively changing. The ocean and temperatures are rising, weather patterns are changing, and human activity is the sole driver in affecting these changes. Overtime the state of the climate naturally changes, but the rate at which things are changing is abnormally fast.

Causes

The main cause of climate change is human actvity.

Greenhouse gases are getting emitted into the air now more than ever. Driving cars, burning fuels, and cutting down forests can all release these emissions. Carbon dioxide, methane, chlorofluorocarbons, water vapor, and nitrous oxide are the main greenhouse gases. When greenhouse gases are released, they act like a heated blanket covering Earth. The gases are basically trapped in the atmosphere, warming Earth.

Effects

Sea Levels are Rising

The world sea level has risen 8 inches within the last hundred years. The Sea level in the United States is said to rise betwwen 1 and 6.6 feet by 2100.

Rising Temperatures

Earth's temperature has been rising over the years. The average temperature of the planet is about 34 degrees fahrenheit warmer than it was in the late 19th century. It's documented that the last decade was the warmest on record. The ocean's are also getting warmer. The top of the ocean has increased about 0.60 degrees fahrenheit since 1969.

Ice Sheets are Melting

Ice sheets in Greenland and Antarctica are melting. The rate at which they are melting has tripled since 2007. Over 200 billion tons of ice each year has melted 1993 and 2019.
